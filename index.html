Picking Time · Difference-in-Differences

Causal evaluation of a Personal Care picking process change at New Cairo FP #4.

Experiment · Significant

Problem

Operations re-organised the Personal Care picking process at New Cairo FP #4. Did the change actually reduce picking time, or were we just riding a general improvement trend?

Approach

Difference-in-Differences regression on per-order picking time, treating Personal Care at FP #4 as the treatment group and Grocery as the parallel-trends control. Robust SEs clustered at the picker level.

Stack

PythonstatsmodelspandasBigQuerymatplotlib

Outcome

−0.48 min / pick (p < 0.001) — the change is causal, not a trend artifact. Rolled out to two more FPs.

Orders Prediction

SARIMAX + STL decomposition forecasts orders 28 days out at sub-area granularity.

Forecast · v12

Problem

Capacity planning meetings were running on gut-feel. We needed a calibrated daily forecast 28 days out that respected weekday seasonality, Ramadan effects, and promotional weeks.

Approach

STL decomposition to isolate trend / seasonal / residual; SARIMAX(1,1,1)(1,1,1,7) with exogenous holiday and promo regressors. Backtested across 90 days with rolling-origin CV.

Stack

PythonstatsmodelsSARIMAXSTLpandasPlotly

Outcome

MAPE 3.8% on a 90-day backtest. Now drives weekly capacity allocation across 17 FPs.

Late-Delivery Retention Paradox

An aggregate chart that looked like a finding — and the Simpson's paradox hiding inside it.

Research · Mar 2026

Problem

An aggregate chart showed customers with 60+ min late deliveries retaining at 85.1% vs on-time at 63.4% — a +21.6 pp gap that looked like late deliveries help retention. Before sharing it, I had to find the confounder.

Approach

Stratified retention by order-frequency × experience bucket. Reading down each column at matched volume, on-time retains as well or better. Cleanest causal cut (new customers only) and a second pass on next-month order frequency among returners surfaced the real signal.

Stack

BigQuery SQLPythonpandasmatplotlibDuckDB

Outcome

It's Simpson's paradox. Heavy users drive both sides — they retain well and they're over-represented in late cohorts. Real damage hides in frequency erosion: on-time returners +38.5% next-month orders, late returners only +3–8%.

Email-to-To-Do Assistant

A small local tool that turns Gmail and Slack DMs into a single manageable reply queue.

Side project · macOS

Problem

Important reply-by emails were getting buried in a busy inbox. I wanted the inbox to behave more like a task list, with deadlines and reminders that survive context switches.

Approach

A local FastAPI + Vue 3 SPA. Polls Gmail read-only and Slack DMs, asks an LLM (Groq · llama-3.3-70b) to detect tasks & extract reply-by dates, persists in SQLite, fires macOS notifications and creates Google Calendar events on a dedicated "Replies" calendar.

Stack

FastAPIVue 3 + TSSQLiteGmail APISlack APIGoogle CalendarGroq · llama-3.3-70b

Outcome

Two inboxes, one queue. Gmail + Slack DMs surfaced as a single ordered task list — no manual triage, $0/month on Groq's free tier.
